About Bukit Timah Hill

Bukit Timah Hill is Singapore's highest natural point and one of the oldest primary rainforests in the country. It is home to a remarkable variety of flora and fauna, many of which are endangered or rare in Southeast Asia.

Threats Facing the Hill

Urban development, pollution, and habitat fragmentation threaten the delicate ecosystem of Bukit Timah Hill. Climate change is also putting stress on biodiversity. Conservation efforts are essential to preserve this vital green space for future generations.
